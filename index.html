Previously founding member of the Alexa LLM post-training team, which became Amazon AGI (led early work on SFT, data collection, and owned RM/eval initiatives). PhD from CMU LTI (advised by Dr. Maxine Eskenazi) working on dialog systems research [thesis] (2022) and BSc from UBC (2018).

As a researcher, I most enjoy doing product-driven research — I strive to do work that enables next-generation user experiences through strong problem formulation (e.g., schemas in my thesis). Key areas include:

  • Compound AI Systems: Agentic workflows, multi-agent evaluation [agentlens '25], multi-step pipelines [structured fusion '19], grounded language models [glm '24], structured representations [schema-guided '21], end-to-end optimization
  • Evaluation: Natural language unit tests [lmunit '24], automatic metrics [usr '20, fed '20], realistic user experience evaluation [nsf report '22, interactive eval track '22, dialport]
  • Retrieval & Ranking: Tournament-based LLM reranking [blitzrank '25], open-weight rerankers
  • Post-training & Alignment: LLM specialization [apo '23], underspecification in alignment [underspec '24]
  • Synthetic Data: Data generation for dialog [lad '22]
Currently focused on multi-agent systems, continuous learning, memory, agentic evaluation, and context representations.

Recent highlights: Our team achieved #1 on the FACTS Grounding leaderboard for factuality, surpassing Gemini 2.5 Pro and GPT-5. We also released open-weight rerankers at the cost/performance frontier and AgentLens, a multi-agent evaluation system.

Previously held internships at Amazon (2018, 2020; improved intent prediction [example-driven '21] and introduced dialoglue), Meta (2016, 2017; shipped first subword neural MT), and Microsoft (2017). Also spent 2 years as a part-time research assistant in bioinformatics at BC Children's Hospital (graph-based genome representation).
